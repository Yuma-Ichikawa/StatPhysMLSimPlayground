\section{Discussion}

\paragraph{What continuation can establish.}
An exact anchor gives semantic meaning to an order parameter, and adjacent deformations
show where that meaning survives. This is stronger than naming visually sharp behavior,
but weaker than a proof of a singular thermodynamic limit. The evidence grades make that
distinction explicit.

\paragraph{Why one paper is useful.}
Transformer representation, generation, diffusion, RL, and deliberation are often discussed
with incompatible meanings of energy, entropy, and temperature. Combining them forces
each quantity to be operational. Optimization temperature is inferred from training
fluctuation and response; diffusion noise indexes a corruption process; policy temperature
regularizes action entropy; generation temperature modifies a categorical sampler. Their
numerical values are never placed on one axis.

\paragraph{Negative results are structural information.}
A broken continuation edge can identify which assumption created a phenomenon. For
example, disappearance under D2 heavy tails implicates moment assumptions; disappearance
under matched-compute debate controls implicates additional inference rather than
communication; and disagreement between guidance response and trajectory commitment
separates static score geometry from reverse dynamics.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
The reduced-order M0--M8 state evolution is an anchor, while the Tier B+ decoder suite performs end-to-end training on a controlled grammar. Neither is labeled as a natural-language Tier C endpoint. Finite MDPs and Gaussian-mixture scores do not contain every feature
of open-ended environments or natural images. Capped numerical representations must be
reported as such when used. Five outer seeds permit auditable intervals but limited tail
resolution. Finally, successful coordinate continuation does not prove shared critical
exponents or universality. Larger sizes, analytic calculations, and independent
implementations remain necessary.

\paragraph{Resource discipline.}
The suite separates GPU-heavy realistic anchors from compact CPU state evolution.
Arrays use all available DGX Spark accelerators for the former and bundle CPU tasks for the latter. Adaptive
follow-up is confined to discovery; confirmatory grids remain immutable. Checkpoint and
artifact levels are chosen so rate functions and trajectory diagnostics can be recomputed
without retaining unnecessary model states.
