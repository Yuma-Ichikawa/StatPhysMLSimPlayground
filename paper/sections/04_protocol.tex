\section{Observables and statistical protocol}
\label{sec:protocol}

\subsection{Functional order parameters}

On a held-out probe set, define pure teacher functions
$F_{\pos}=\bA\bX$ and $F_{\sem}=\bS^*(\bX)\bX$.  After centering and unit-norm
normalizing the vectorized outputs and templates, we solve
\begin{equation}
  \widehat F = \mpos \widehat F_{\pos}
             + \msem \widehat F_{\sem}+R
  \label{eq:functional-decomposition}
\end{equation}
by least squares.  We report both partial coefficients, marginal overlaps,
$\|R\|^2$, $R^2$, and the template condition number.  Coefficients are not
clipped to $[-1,1]$: correlated templates can produce larger partial
coefficients, in which case the conditioning diagnostic prevents an
overconfident phase label.  A conservative classifier returns positional,
semantic, coexistence, disordered/unresolved, or unresolved.

\subsection{Mechanistic instrument panel}

For each layer/head, effective QK and OV operators are retained with their
singular spectra, stable ranks, leading gaps, and planted-subspace principal
angles.  Head--latent overlap matrices are matched by a maximum-weight
assignment, with permutation-invariant singular spectra, specialization
entropy, effective head count, and dead/redundant-head fractions.  Attention
geometry includes entropy, effective support, span, diagonal, previous-token,
and sink mass.  Hidden states yield covariance participation ratio, effective
rank, anisotropy, position correlations, and correlation length.  Removing
attention or the MLP provides a causal loss delta rather than a purely
correlational attribution.

To test whether a spectral outlier precedes semantic order, QK diagnostics and
$(\mpos,\msem)$ are sampled on the same registered trajectory.  Onset rules are
fixed before confirmatory inspection and uncertainty is bootstrapped over
teachers, data sets, and optimization replicas.  A lead/lag claim requires a
confidence interval on the onset difference, not visual ordering of two
curves.

\subsection{Fluctuations and finite-size evidence}

Let $m$ denote a scalar order parameter over independent replicas.  We measure
\begin{align}
  \chi_N &= N_{\rm eff}\left(\langle m^2\rangle-\langle m\rangle^2\right),\\
  U_4 &= 1-\frac{\langle m^4\rangle}{3\langle m^2\rangle^2},\\
  I_N(m)&=-N_{\rm eff}^{-1}\log P_N(m)+\mathrm{const}.
\end{align}
The effective size $N_{\rm eff}$ is specified by the scaling path rather than
inferred from array length.  We retain $P_N(m)$ to diagnose bimodality and
barrier growth, use matched forward/backward initialization scans for
hysteresis, and locate Binder crossings by pairwise interpolation without
silently discarding multiple crossings.

For a continuous-transition candidate we test
\begin{equation}
  m(N,g)=N^{-\beta/\nu}
  \mathcal F\!\left((g-g_c)N^{1/\nu}\right)
  \label{eq:fss}
\end{equation}
using a declared exponent grid, interpolation-aware collapse score, and
hierarchical bootstrap.  A distribution-shift peak or maximum slope proposes
a region for refinement; it never alone upgrades the language from
``crossover'' to ``transition.''

\subsection{Replication, exploration, and artifacts}

Randomness is split into teacher, quenched data distribution, initialization,
minibatch order, and dropout streams derived from a root seed.  Estimation is
nested in that order.  Treating optimization replicas as independent teachers
would understate uncertainty, so confidence intervals use the same hierarchy.

Exploratory manifests localize candidate regions and may propose a new,
separately saved refinement manifest.  Confirmatory grids are content-addressed
before their outcomes are aggregated.  Each run directory contains its exact
specification, Git and accelerator provenance, status/attempt history,
checksummed trajectories and diagnostics, teacher state, and a compact
summary.  Missing and failed runs remain visible.  A run is claim-eligible only
after schema, status, required-artifact, checksum, provenance, and finite-value
validation.

The five headline hypotheses, their observables, controls, and rejection
conditions are machine-readable in \code{experiments/transformer\_phase\_atlas/claims.toml}.
