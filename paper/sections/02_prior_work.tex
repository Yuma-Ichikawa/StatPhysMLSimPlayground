\section{Closest prior work and the unresolved gap}

Solvable attention already predicts a positional--semantic transition in a
tied low-rank layer~\cite{cui2024phase}, while attention-indexed models extend
closed-form predictions to full-width matrices~\cite{boncoraglio2026aim}.
Thus observing a transition or a spectral outlier is not our novelty; the test
is transport to a hidden deformation.  Diffusion theory separates speciation
from memorized-point collapse~\cite{biroli2024dynamical}, and recent DiT
experiments report near-concurrent symmetry breaking and nonlocality
onset~\cite{zhang2026concurrence}.  We therefore predict relative critical
times rather than rediscovering these diagnostics.

In RL, policy-entropy collapse has a mechanistic account and targeted controls
already exist~\cite{cui2025entropy}; proxy optimization can degrade gold
quality as KL budget grows~\cite{rafailov2024overoptimization}.  Entropy alone
is consequently not an order parameter.  In multi-agent systems, global-flip
experiments show that apparent consensus can be field-driven rather than
interaction-driven~\cite{denobili2026collective}.  Effective-team-size laws
and matched coordination controls further show that nominal agent count is
not independent evidence~\cite{bertalanic2026ringelmann,kim2026scalingagents}.
