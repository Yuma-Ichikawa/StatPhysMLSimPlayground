\section{Theory frontier and the unresolved gap}
\label{sec:frontier}

The taxonomy is our organizational proposal, informed by reproducibility
frameworks that make model and dataset assumptions explicit
\cite{mitchell2019modelcards,gebru2021datasheets,pineau2021reproducibility}.
It is not presented as an established consensus.

For attention, a tied low-rank model exhibits a positional--semantic transition
\cite{cui2024phase}, and attention-indexed models extend Bayes predictions to
full-width matrices \cite{boncoraglio2026aim}. The open question is therefore
not whether simplified attention can transition, but whether its boundary and
order semantics survive standard blocks, MLPs, normalization, causal
objectives, optimization, and natural carriers.

Diffusion theory distinguishes speciation from later memorized-point collapse
\cite{biroli2024dynamical}; DDPM and DiT provide the relevant learned endpoints
\cite{ho2020ddpm,peebles2023dit}. A recent unreviewed preprint reports nearby
symmetry-breaking and nonlocality onsets \cite{zhang2026concurrence}, which we
treat as a hypothesis rather than settled evidence. In RL, proxy optimization
can reduce gold quality \cite{gao2023reward,rafailov2024overoptimization}, so
entropy must be read jointly with gold reward and strategy support. Consensus
dynamics have a classical foundation \cite{degroot1974consensus}; modern MARL
and LLM debate add nonstationarity and heterogeneous language agents
\cite{lowe2017maddpg,du2024debate}. The 2026 statistical-physics agent papers
we cite are explicitly recent preprints, useful for hypotheses but not a mature
theoretical frontier \cite{denobili2026collective,bertalanic2026ringelmann}.
