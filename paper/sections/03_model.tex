\section{Controlled teacher--student system}
\label{sec:model}

\subsection{Exact bridge}

Let $\bX\in\R^{L\times d}$ contain raw token rows
$\bx_\ell\sim\mathcal N(0,\sigma^2 I_d)$.  The exact bridge uses $L=2$,
$\sigma=0.5$, one planted semantic vector $\bq^*\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$,
and antipodal position shifts
\begin{equation}
  \bp_1=\frac{\bm 1}{\sqrt d},\qquad
  \bp_2=-\frac{\bm 1}{\sqrt d},\qquad
  \widetilde{\bX}=\bX+\bP.
\end{equation}
The semantic teacher attention and fixed positional operator are
\begin{align}
  \bS^*(\bX)
  &=\softmax_{\rm row}\!\left[
    \frac{(\bX\bq^*)(\bX\bq^*)^\top}{d}\right],\\
  \bA&=\begin{pmatrix}0.6&0.4\\0.4&0.6\end{pmatrix}.
\end{align}
For positional mixture $\omega\in[0,1]$, targets are generated from the
\emph{raw} tokens,
\begin{equation}
  \bY(\bX)=\left[(1-\omega)\bS^*(\bX)+\omega\bA\right]\bX.
  \label{eq:teacher}
\end{equation}
The $M0$ student sees shifted tokens and learns a tied rank-$r$ factor
$\bQ\in\R^{d\times r}$,
\begin{equation}
  f_{\bQ}(\widetilde{\bX})=
  \softmax_{\rm row}\!\left[
    \frac{(\widetilde{\bX}\bQ)(\widetilde{\bX}\bQ)^\top}{d}\right]
  \widetilde{\bX}.
  \label{eq:student}
\end{equation}
For $n$ examples, the calibration objective exactly preserves the reference
normalization,
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal L(\bQ)=\frac{1}{2d}\sum_{\mu=1}^{n}
  \left\|f_{\bQ}(\widetilde{\bX}_\mu)-\bY_\mu\right\|_F^2
  +\frac{\lambda}{2}\|\bQ\|_F^2,
  \label{eq:loss}
\end{equation}
rather than a framework-default elementwise mean.  This distinction matters:
dividing by $n$ would change the regularizer's relative strength as
$n=\alpha d$ is varied.  We reproduce the reference informed semantic and
positional initializations as well as random initialization.

\subsection{Architecture ladder}

Table~\ref{tab:architecture} defines cumulative stages.  Every stage exposes
the same output, attention, representation, effective-QK, effective-OV, and
ablation interface.  $M8$ adds a pointwise autoregressive readout and evaluates
predictions at positions $1{:}L-1$ against targets $2{:}L$; a causal-invariance
test ensures that changing future tokens cannot alter earlier outputs.

\subsection{Data ladder}

The teacher task and seed hierarchy remain fixed while the raw-token ensemble
changes: $D0$ isotropic Gaussian; $D1$ elliptical Gaussian with recorded
covariance and condition number; $D2$ variance-normalized Student-$t$;
$D3$ a quenched discrete codebook; $D4$ a latent Markov chain with recorded
transition and emission matrices; and $D5$ a fixed-length binary probabilistic
grammar with recorded rule tensor, leaf nonterminals, and emissions.  The
teacher can be configured to act on raw or shifted tokens, but the exact bridge
locks both the semantic logits and target values to raw tokens.

\subsection{Thermodynamic scaling paths}

A run records
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal P=(d,L,H,d_h,K,r,d_{\rm ff},V,n),\qquad
  n=\lceil c d^\gamma\rceil .
\end{equation}
We separately test $\gamma=1$ and $\gamma=2$; fixed-$d_h$ and fixed-$H$ paths;
and fixed versus growing depth.  Results from different paths are not pooled
into one critical exponent.  Parameter count and effective sample ratio are
saved for every point.
