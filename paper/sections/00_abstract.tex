\begin{abstract}
We formulate a single numerical research program for the thermodynamics of modern learning
systems. The organizing principle is \emph{phase continuation}: begin with a model whose
oracle, symmetry, or state evolution is controlled, then add architectural and algorithmic
ingredients one at a time while retaining matched latent structure and common observables.
The program joins a Transformer ladder M0--M8 and data ladder D0--D5 to diffusion models,
reinforcement learning, multi-agent deliberation, mixture-of-experts routing, retrieval,
multimodal alignment, and compression. It deliberately distinguishes optimization
temperature, attention temperature, diffusion noise, policy entropy, and generation
temperature. Across domains we measure recovery, specialization, effective multiplicity,
interaction range, macrostate entropy, replica overlap, generalization response, and
information load. Claims require exactly \NSeeds{} outer seeds, nested quenched/annealed
uncertainty, six or more sizes, susceptibility and Binder diagnostics, hysteresis controls,
and explicit finite-size corrections. A machine-readable coverage registry links every
claim to a runner, configuration, observable, and falsifier. The accompanying implementation
therefore serves both as a paper and as an auditable protocol: numerical values enter the
manuscript only after all five replicas are complete. We do not assume that the four domains
share a universality class; the test is whether phase structure continues under controlled
deformation, and where that continuation fails.
\end{abstract}
