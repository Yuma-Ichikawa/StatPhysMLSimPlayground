\begin{abstract}
A solvable model of dot-product attention exhibits a transition between
positional and semantic solutions, but it is unknown which parts of this
phenomenon survive the ingredients that distinguish a Transformer from the
solvable model.  We turn this gap into a controlled numerical program.  We
first reproduce the exact two-token, tied low-rank teacher--student bridge,
then add one ingredient at a time: untied queries and keys, trainable
QKVO projections, multiple heads, residual paths and normalization, an MLP,
causality and rotary position embeddings, depth, and an autoregressive
objective.  Orthogonally, we replace isotropic Gaussian tokens by elliptical,
heavy-tailed, codebook, hidden-Markov, and grammar-generated sequences.  A
common instrument panel measures functional positional/semantic order,
permutation-invariant head specialization, QK/OV spectra, representation
geometry, interventions, replica overlaps, susceptibilities, Binder
cumulants, and finite-size collapse.  Every run is an immutable,
content-addressed unit with independent teacher, data, initialization,
minibatch, and dropout seeds; exploratory and confirmatory manifests are
separate.  At manuscript generation time the registered experiment status is
\emph{\ResultStatus} (\NumCompletedRuns{} completed runs).  This design makes
three outcomes scientifically useful: a universal boundary would identify a
robust macroscopic mechanism, systematic drift would delimit the solvable
theory's domain, and disappearance would locate the architectural ingredient
that destroys the transition.  We release the complete implementation,
Slurm workflow, raw-data contract, and claim-level statistical tests.
\end{abstract}
