\section{Relation to prior work}

\paragraph{Statistical mechanics of learning.}
Replica, cavity, and state-evolution methods connect generalization to overlaps and free
energies in high-dimensional inference \citep{mezard1987spin,zdeborova2016statistical}.
Neural tangent and mean-field limits provide different controlled endpoints
\citep{jacot2018neural}. Our focus is numerical continuation between endpoints rather than
a claim that one asymptotic theory describes every model. Binder cumulants and finite-size
crossings are borrowed from lattice practice \citep{binder1981critical}, but are accompanied
by correction-to-scaling and crossover checks.

\paragraph{Scaling and emergence in language models.}
Empirical scaling laws relate loss to model, data, and compute
\citep{kaplan2020scaling,hoffmann2022training}. In-context learning has been interpreted
as implicit regression or optimization \citep{akyurek2023learning,vonoswald2023transformers}.
Mechanistic work identifies attention heads and circuits with specialized functions
\citep{elhage2021mathematical}. These results motivate our functional overlaps, head
symmetry diagnostics, and context-length scaling, but do not by themselves establish a
thermodynamic transition.

\paragraph{Modern system ingredients.}
Low-rank adaptation \citep{hu2022lora}, sparse expert routing
\citep{shazeer2017outrageously,fedus2022switch}, retrieval augmentation
\citep{lewis2020retrieval}, and multimodal contrastive learning
\citep{radford2021learning} introduce new ranks, occupancies, and multiplicities.
Compression and quantized adaptation can alter those quantities after training
\citep{dettmers2023qlora}. We treat these as lifecycle deformations of one registered
order-parameter system.

\paragraph{Diffusion, control, and collective reasoning.}
Denoising diffusion and score-based models define a noise-indexed family of distributions
\citep{ho2020denoising,song2021score}; classifier-free guidance supplies a response
intervention \citep{ho2022classifierfree}. Maximum-entropy control and preference-based
learning provide analogous policy-level controls
\citep{sutton2018reinforcement,christiano2017deep}. Debate and communication protocols
motivate controlled multi-agent interventions \citep{irving2018ai}. Our cross-domain tests
ask whether shared coordinates persist under matched latent ensembles, not whether these
algorithms are literally the same physical system.
