\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We converted one solvable attention transition into a falsifiable research
program for Transformer thermodynamics.  The exact bridge, architecture and
data ladders, functional order parameters, mechanistic probes, finite-size
tests, and registered artifact contract make it possible to say precisely
what survives, what shifts, and what disappears as realism is added.  Just as
importantly, the protocol defines when the evidence does \emph{not} justify
phase-transition language.

The next theoretical task depends on the numerical outcome.  Robust collapse
onto a shared boundary would motivate a coarse-grained order-parameter theory
that is invariant to heads and layers.  Stage-specific drift would call for an
expanded phase card including normalization, optimization, and scaling path.
Loss of the phenomenon at a particular rung would identify the minimal model
that theory must solve next.  The released playground is organized for this
iteration: new models, data ensembles, observables, cluster profiles, and
claims can be added independently while every paper result remains tied to an
immutable manifest.
