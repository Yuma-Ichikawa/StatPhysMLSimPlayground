\section{Conclusion}

Predictive phase continuation asks whether a solvable model forecasts an
unseen boundary, not whether two curves look alike.  The framework makes
failure local and useful: non-additive assumption interactions identify where
transport breaks, and an additional macrovariable must recover prediction on
the same blinded system.  Nested disorder, competing transition models,
adaptive boundary replication, and critical-window interventions convert a
large atlas into four falsifiable claims.  The controlled benchmark is a step
toward, rather than a substitute for, natural-data external validation.
